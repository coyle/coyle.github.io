3 Goals for 2015

Dennis Coyle

As the year winds down, it is time to start reflecting on 2014;

what went right, what went wrong and what should be changed in 2015?

My list of three goals for 2015:

1. Eat healthier

To keep the body in good health is a duty... otherwise we shall not be able to keep our mind strong and clear. - Buddha

I can't point at 2014 and say that this year, has been particularly devasating to my health. There has been a slow and steady decline, over a period of a few years, in the amount of effort and significance I have put into my physical well being. I want to make a lasting change in the foods I consume. In the past, following a particular diet has not been sustainable. This time around, I will refrain from eating foods that have the largest negative impact on my health.

  1. Red Meats
  2. Dairy
  3. Processed Foods
2. Make my family a priority

“Action expresses priorities.” ― Mahatma Gandhi

I am very intentional with my personal development and I am lucky enough to have a wife that fully supports and believes in me. Unfortunately, I have not been very intentional with the development of my marriage. With all the change 2014 brought, I took for granted how special my wife is to me. So 2015 will be a 180° from 2014 in regards to my family. My wife's love languages are acts of service and quality time. So in 2015, I will write her one letter a week, fully plan one special night a week and will take care of dinners.

3. Journal every single day

“Writing is the only way I have to explain my own life to myself.” ― Pat Conroy

I have this goal more from curiosity than any specific outcome. I want to answer the question "What changes will occur from a daily habit of writing my thoughts and actions down".
