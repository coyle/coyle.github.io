Simplify

Dennis Coyle

Truth is ever to be found in the simplicity, and not in the multiplicity and confusion of things.”

― Isaac Newton

The past three months have been a whirlwind for my family. My son Jackson was born nine weeks ago today. With Jackson, came an upheaval of our daily routines, lack of sleep and an abundance of uncertainty surrounding my parenting abilities. As the chaos ensued, I struggled to keep the majority of my pre fatherhood responsibilities and routines in place. I maintained everything, yet I was not quite able to do any single thing well.

Two days ago I read a wonderful blog post from my friend Kyle Van Pelt. In the post kyle discusses how to use the same strategies one would use in the game of Risk, towards building out a great personal network. The main strategy being, fortifying your core before trying to expand.

As with most things in life, I read this strategy in the context of my current place in life. I have added on a great deal of responsibility and I have added it onto a very core part; my family. If a core aspect of life is in turmoil, everything else on the periphery will suffer. Conversely, if everything on the periphery is in upheaval yet the core is stable and well reinforced, life will be viable. As simple and intuitive as this is, I had completely missed it.

Therefore, this is one of those times that I need to strategically retreat, focus on rebuilding the core and simplify the periphery.
